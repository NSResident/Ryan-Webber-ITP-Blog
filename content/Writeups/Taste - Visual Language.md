Where is the line between taste as conformity and genuine internal reaction to something?

Algorithmically defined taste comes from a broad acceptance of ratings and polls, not by curators, but by the every day person (rotten tomatoes, spotify, netflix, good reads, etc.)
- But why is this prevalent?
	- Addictive technology
	- Desperation to be average
	- Acceptance of taste (as in liking what is known to be tasteful) as a marker of cultural understanding
	- Makes discovery easy (even if the quality of discovery isnt the same as finding it yourself, "good enough")
	- (BIGGEST QUESTION MARK) Wanting to get your money's worth and not waste time on something you don't enjoy, or maybe deeply understood as not being able to see the value in disagreement
		- A bad book can be a good book if it makes you think or feel. In critique and dispute comes strengthening of what you like and a deeper understanding of what you don't
		- The goal is to both find what fits you and increasing your understanding of the area (which can only happen outside an echo chamber). Algorithms will only do the first, and poorly at that (theres no story behind your discovery, and to humans, the story is everything)
