

#### [*40 Part Motet* by Janet Cardiff](https://www.youtubeeducation.com/watch?v=ncWFLzVrwU4)

**Visual**
40 speakers arranged in a facing inwards. At the center of circles are two parallel benches. The speakers are raised on stands above the listener. There are multiple singers, some are in completely unison others are slightly faster or slower, but all it sounds as if all are singing at the same time

**Effect of the Design**
By placing the listener in the middle of the circle and having the speakers project inwards, they are immersed in the song and all its variations simultaneously. It gives the feeling of choir singing at you rather than with you. The height of the speakers help with this by completely projecting the sound to the listener. The two parallel benches provide enough seating for a small group, which opens the possibility for a communal experience without crowding the center.

**Meaning (w/o research)**
Perhaps Janet Cardiff wanted to demonstrate the barrier between a song and the world by cutting off the outside (surrounding the listener in a circle). The song sounds old and very Christian, so the layering of multiple renditions of the same song could also display it's timeliness as it has survived through the ages. Circles can be used as a way to represent time as a loop where the past influences the future which in turn takes from the past. 

**Creative Process**
1. Mid 1500s song called Spem in Alium by Thomas Tallis, 40 different harmonies built into the song its really 40 people singing one song
2. Speakers are grouped up by choir groups to represent the different ranges that sing the song (bass, alto, tenor, etc.)
3. The intent was a 3D sound that flows from one speaker group to the other like ripples
4. Daily conversation and life also recorded and played for the exhibit, humanizing and then deifying the singers (came up with the idea on set)

#### [*Dawn Chorus by Marcus Coates*](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qEso2yDIZEU)
[Another Link](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zF1uihdcZmY)

**Visual**
Series of sped up videos where people are imitating bird songs. The people are in mundane environments (office, home, etc.) doing mundane tasks such as reading a newspaper. The birdsongs include very high pitches and sharp squawking. There are 14 videos in total and all could be viewed simultaneously within the exhibits, with the screens at varying heights.

**Effect of the Design**
There is a surrealist feel to videos as you see a man or woman but hear a bird song that couldn't possible have come from them. At the time of watching the origin of the song is ambigous: Is it a birdsong recorded over a sped up video or the sped up video causing the bird song? The exhibit allows you to view all videos simultaneously and they are raised well above eye level, both of which play into theme of hearing birds, knowing they are above, and not seeing them. The use of mundane settings and the everyday person also invokes a suspension of disbelief to thinking they are normally conversing in a birdsong and that this is normal and accepted.

**Meaning (w/o research)**
Perhaps Marcus Coates wanted to show the similarities between  birds and humans through musical pitch and range to highlight structural similarities. There could also be a double meaning in using daily life and actives as the setting to indicate that these actions and places are often treated as mostly inconsequential in ones life and, like the bird songs we hear but don't listen to everyday, fade into the background. 

[**Creative Process**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zF1uihdcZmY)
1. Record different birds by placing microphones where birds could be located
	1. Can control and process multiple microphones simultaneously
2. Visited choirs and asked "Who wants to sing like a bird?"
3. Auditioned to match which species of bird to the choir member
4. Have them sing the birdsong in slow motion for 2 hours and use audio processing to speed it up
5. Videos reflect where each choir member spent their time
6. In exhibit, the screens were placed to roughly reflect the height of the mics
7. Exhibit was in a church, which gave a sense of openness, both specially and critically, and creates an environment where it is accepted to just listen and observe without judgement
